Nouns

  • Common/Proper

    Common Nouns

    Common nouns identify any person, place, thing or idea or unnamed nouns.
    Examples: computer, car, game, girl, love, freedom

    Proper Nouns

    Proper nouns identify a specific person, place, thing or idea or named noun.
    Examples: Ford Explorer, Lord of the Rings, Brad Pitt, Indianapolis

  • Abstract/Concrete

    Abstract

    Abstract nouns identify ideas, feelings, or qualities that can not be seen or touched.
    Examples: love, hate, courage, bravery, fear, history, truth.

    Concrete

    Concrete nouns identify nouns you can see, taste, hear, smell or touch
    Examples: car, book, movie, actor, mayor, athlete, city

  • Plural

    Plural

    Plural nouns identify one or more nouns.
    Examples: games, vehicles, keys, cities
    For most plural nouns you simply add -s, but for others you may add -es or -ies (see chart)

    Nouns ending in:

    Add

    Example

    -s,-ss, -zz, -ch, -sh, -x

     add -es

    buses, buzzes, foxes

    -o preceded by a vowel

     add -s

    studios, stereos

    -o preceded by a consonant

     

    Usually add -es

    heroes, potatoes

    Sometimes add -s

    zeros, photos, pianos

    -y, preceded by a vowel

    add -s

    days, donkeys, keys

    -y preceded by a consonant

    usually change -y to -i and add -es

    cities, dairies, pennies

    -f or -fe

    Usually change -f to -v and add -es

    leaves, lives, calves

    Sometimes add -s

    roofs, surfs, beliefs
  • Possessive

    Possessive

    Possessive nouns show ownership or possession
    Examples: dog's collar, class' homework
    To form the possessive form of a noun, the writer must add either an 's or simply an ' depending on how that noun ends.

    Nouns ending in:

    Add

    Example

    Most singular nouns

    add 's

    John's books are new.

    Singular nouns ending in "s"

    add 's

    The bus's tires are flat.

    Plural nouns ending in "s"

    add an '

    The heroes' banquet is tonight at 9:00.

    Plural nouns not ending in "s"

    Sometimes add 's

    The men's room is located on the 3rd floor.
  • Collective

    Collective

    Collective nouns identify groups of people or things.
    Examples:
    Most collective nouns are singular; however, collective nouns can take either a singular or plural verb depending on how it is used in the sentence.
    The noun is singular when all members of the group act as a single unit, but it is plural if they do not act as a single unit.

    For example:
    (singular) The team shares the field with its opponent.
    (singular) The committee discusses their reactions.
    (plural) The committee disagree among themselves.

  • Appositive

    Appositive

    Appositives, or Appositive phrases, rename nouns to give a clear understanding of the identification of the noun.
    Examples:

    The noun is singular when all members of the group act as a single unit, but it is plural if they do not act as a single unit.

    For example:
    (singular) The team shares the field with its opponent.
    (singular) The committee discusses their reactions.
    (plural) The committee disagree among themselves.
