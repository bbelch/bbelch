Prepostions

  • Definition

    Prepositions are words that we use to tell the "position" of a noun or where a verb takes place.
    For example: He stood cautiously on the tight rope.
    She skillfully navigated over the river and through the woods.

    There are four aspects to studying prepositions

    Prepositons
    Prepositions words that link a noun or pronoun to another word in the sentence
    Prepositional Phrase a phrase that begins with a preposition and ends with its object (called the object of the preposition)
    Object of the Preposition the noun or pronoun following the preposition.
    Adjective Phrase prepositional phrase that modifies a noun or a pronoun
    Adverbial Phrase prepositional phrase that modifies a verb

  • Phrase

    Prepositional Phrase

    Prepositions are added to another word or object of the preposition (noun or pronoun) to form prepositional phrases.
    The phrases begin with a preposition and ends with the first noun or pronoun that follows it.  That noun or pronoun is called the Object of the preposition

    For example:
    The computer on the desk actually sits on top of a stand.

    In the above sentence the prepositional phrase "on the desk" begins with the preposition "on" and ends with the object "desk" while the phrase "on top of a stand" begins with the compound preposition "on top of" and ends with the object "stand".

    Sometimes a writer may use several prepositional phrases in a row. They are considered separate though they may seem to perform the same function.

    For example:
    During the beginning of the show, most performers are nervous and jittery.

    In the above sentence the prepositional phrase "during the beginning" and "of the show" are two separate phrases. "During the beginning" is actually an adverbial phrase modifying the verb "are" while the phrase "of the show" is an adjective preposition modifying the object of the preposition "beginning".

    Remember, when using pronouns as objects of the preposition you must use Object Pronouns.

  • Object

    Object of the Preposition

    The object of the preposition is the first noun or pronoun that acts like a noun or pronoun following the preposition.
    The "OP" is the word that is being linked to the another word.

    For example:
    Sitting at the computer desk, the boys played minecraft for an hour.

    In the above sentence the prepositional phrase "at the computer desk" begins with the preposition "at" and ends with the object "desk".
    That object "desk" is being linked to the participle "Sitting".

  • Adjective

    Adjective Phrase

    an Adjective Phrase is a prepositional phrase becomes an adjective phrase when it modifies a noun or a pronoun.

    For example:
    Someone from the office dropped off the copies.
    Many students at Decatur Discovery Academy have at least one computer at home.

    In the first sentence, the phrase "from the office" describes the Indefinite pronoun "Someone" therefore by definition it is an adjective. 
    In the second sentence, the phrase "at Decatur Discovery Academy" describes the noun "students", once again making the phrase an adjective, while "at least one computer" is an adverbial phrase modifying "have" and "at home" modifies "computer" acting as an adjective.

  • Adverbial

    Adverbial Phrase

    an Adverb Phrase is a prepositional phrase that acts as an adverb phrase when it modifies a verb, adjective, or adverb.

    For example:
    The student fell in the stairwell.    
    Though he sometimes walked to school, Garrett preferred to ride his bike.

    In the first sentence, the phrase "in the stairwell" describes the verb "fell" (telling where he fell); therefore, by definition it is an adverb. 
    In the next sentence, the phrase "to school" explains where he "walked".
